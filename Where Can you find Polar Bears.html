Where to see Polar bears in the Wild

Churchill, Manitoba, Canada Svalbard, Norway Kaktovik, Alaska Wrangel Island, Russia
Churchill Wild is an Arctic safari outfitter
with tours ranging from photography-focused
excursions to lodging in luxury
while spotting the great white bears.		 The Norwegian archipelago of Svalbard
is very far north in Greenland.
It’s a great place to see polar bears from a ship
rather than on land		 Akook Arctic Adventures is an indigenous-run
outfitter in Kaktovik that offers
polar bear tours by boat.
Polar bears gather here in significant
numbers, and that combined with
local expertise makes for a
really good chance at bear sightings.		 Wrangel Island is about as far east as you can go
in Russia before hitting Alaska.
The plus side of this isolation
is that there are not many people
around, so wildlife is able to flourish,
polar bears included.
